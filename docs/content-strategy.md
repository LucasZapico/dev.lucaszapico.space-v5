# How I Work

I'm not an agency. I'm one person with strong opinions about architecture and a bias toward systems that business owners can actually control.

**Process section:**

## The Process

**1. Understand the real problem**
Most projects start with a solution in mind. I start with constraints — what does your business actually need to operate? What's creating friction? What decisions need to happen before anyone writes code?

**2. Architecture before interface**
I choose infrastructure based on your constraints: self-hosted when you need data control, headless CMS when your team needs to publish without a developer, managed services when operational simplicity matters more than customization.

**3. Build, ship, iterate**
I build in working increments — not wireframes followed by months of silence. You see real software early and often.

**4. Hand off something that lasts**
Documentation, CMS training, deployment pipelines. The system works without me. That's the point.

**Tooling philosophy section:**

## Tools & Approach

I use whatever gets the best result for the problem at hand.

**Current stack:** React Router / Remix, NestJS, PostgreSQL, Directus CMS, Medusa (headless commerce), Stripe, Docker, Tailwind CSS, TypeScript across the board.

**On AI-assisted development:** I use AI tools throughout my workflow — research, code generation, testing, documentation. This isn't a shortcut; it's how I deliver the throughput of a small team as a solo practitioner. The architecture, client understanding, and system design are mine. The tools I use to execute are whatever gets the best result.

**What this means for you:** Lower cost, faster timelines, and direct communication with the person building your system. No project managers, no handoffs, no translation layers.

**Who it's for section:**

## Who I Work With

- Business owners who need custom systems, not templates
- Operators who've outgrown their current tooling
- Teams where off-the-shelf software creates more problems than it solves
- Anyone who values discretion, reliability, and working directly with the builder

## When Nobody's Paying

The side projects are where the real curiosity lives:
